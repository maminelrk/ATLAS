### Imperial Cities of Fez and Meknes

**Fez (Fès):**
Fez is Morocco's spiritual and cultural capital, founded in the 8th century. It is home to **Al-Qarawiyyin University**, which is considered the oldest continually operating educational institution in the world.
* **Fes el Bali:** The oldest walled part of the city, a vast, car-free urban area and UNESCO World Heritage Site containing winding alleyways and historical landmarks.
* **Chouara Tannery:** The largest and oldest tannery in Fes, famous for its large stone vats filled with colorful dyes. Visitors are often given mint leaves to help with the strong smell.
* Fez was the capital of Morocco during the Marinid dynasty.

**Meknes:**
Meknes is another imperial city known for its vast historical monuments and massive walls. It was the capital of Morocco under Sultan Moulay Ismail (1672–1727).
* **Bab Mansour:** Recognized as one of the most beautiful gates in North Africa, featuring intricate zellij tiling and carvings.
* **Heri es-Souani:** Massive historical granaries and stables built by Moulay Ismail to house thousands of horses.
